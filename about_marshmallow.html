All about marshmallows

...and how we make them.

Marshmallow (French zéphir ← Latin zephyrus ← ancient Greek ζέφῠρος - western or north-western wind) is a kind of sugary confectionery based on fruits and berries.

It is made by churning fruit puree with sugar and egg white, followed by adding some form-forming (gelling) filler to the mixture: pectin, agar syrup, gelatin (marmalade) mass.

The prototype of marshmallow was “pastila”, which was made in ancient Russia from apples and honey. French confectioners replaced honey with sugar and gave the pastille the shape that we know now.
